Miyawaki Methodology

Grow dense, diverse forests in years, not decades

What is Miyawaki Method?

The Miyawaki Method is a technique for creating dense, native forests in a relatively short period. Developed by Japanese botanist Akira Miyawaki in the 1970s, it has gained popularity worldwide for its effectiveness in restoring degraded land and promoting biodiversity.

Benefits of the Miyawaki Method

Faster Growth

By mimicking natural forest succession, Miyawaki forests can grow 10 times faster and be 30 times denser than traditionally planted forests.

Increased Biodiversity

Using native species creates a habitat for native wildlife and promotes a healthy ecosystem.

Improved Soil Health

Dense planting encourages competition for light, leading to faster growth and improved soil quality.

Climate Change Mitigation

Rapidly growing forests absorb more carbon dioxide, helping to combat climate change.

Urban Greening

The method can be used in small spaces, making it ideal for creating urban forests and green spaces.

Implementing the Miyawaki Method

Step 1: Site Preparation

Choose a location and assess soil quality. Amending the soil with organic matter is often necessary.

Step 2: Species Selection

Identify native tree, shrub, and under-story plant species that would naturally occur in the area.

Step 3: Dense Planting

Plant a high number of seedlings (30,000-50,000 per hectare) close together, mimicking natural forest density.

Step 4: Maintainance

Regular weeding and watering are crucial for the first 2-3 years to establish the forest.

Step 5: Minimal Intervention

Once established, Miyawaki forests require minimal maintenance and can thrive on their own.
